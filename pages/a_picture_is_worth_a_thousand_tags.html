A picture is worth a thousand tags

Ok, here's an idea. Let's say you want to have a nice diagram with both graphics and numbers. Something between a picture and a table. The usual way to make it would be to draw numbers on an image and display that. But there is an alternative route. You can first turn your picture into a table, and then populate this table with your data.

The second part is hard to show without actual data, so here's the demo for the first one.

Here is a canvas. You can load your own picture, or use Ctrl+V to copy one from the clipboard, or just let it be. Then click the “canvas to textarea” button and the text area gets populated by the table's source code. There will appear an example of the table below the area, too.






This lets you chimerize tables and images just right but, on the downside, this is a terribly inefficient way to encode pictures. I'm planning to use it to create diagrams for this site. I already had a need for that and went for drawing simple plots with canvas' turtle graphics which is a little tedious. But if turtle graphics appears to be more economical, I might still stick to that.

After all, Words and Buttons is all about instant-loading self-sufficient small pages.
